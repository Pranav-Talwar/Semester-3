Dhanurasana (The Bow Pose)

Dhanurasana, commonly known as the Bow Pose, is an invigorating backbend that helps open up the chest and strengthen the spine.

  • Stretches the entire front body, including the chest, abdomen, and thighs.
  • Strengthens the back muscles and improves posture.
  • Boosts digestion and stimulates abdominal organs.
  • Relieves stress and fatigue by rejuvenating the body.
  • Improves flexibility and mobility in the spine and hips.
  • Step 1: Begin by lying flat on your stomach, arms resting alongside your body, and legs stretched out.
  • Step 2: Bend your knees and bring your heels close to your buttocks. Reach back with your hands to hold your ankles.
  • Step 3: Inhale deeply and lift your chest and thighs off the ground, pulling your ankles upwards with your hands.
  • Step 4: Hold the pose for 15â€“30 seconds while breathing steadily, feeling the stretch in your body.
  • Step 5: To release, exhale and gently lower your chest and legs back to the mat.
  • Step 6: Rest for a few moments before repeating or transitioning to another pose.

Note: Avoid this pose if you have recent back or neck injuries. Practice with care and focus on your breathing to ease into the stretch.
