Deadlift

The Deadlift is a full-body compound exercise that targets the back, glutes, hamstrings, and core, making it one of the most effective movements for building strength and power.

  • Builds total-body strength by engaging multiple muscle groups.
  • Improves posture and strengthens the posterior chain.
  • Enhances grip strength and functional fitness.
  • Boosts metabolism and promotes fat loss through high energy expenditure.
  • Strengthens the core for better balance and stability.
  • Step 1: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and the barbell close to your shins. Keep your back straight.
  • Step 2: Bend at your hips and knees, gripping the bar with your hands slightly outside your legs.
  • Step 3: Inhale, brace your core, and push through your heels to lift the bar, keeping it close to your body.
  • Step 4: Stand tall at the top of the lift with your shoulders back, avoiding overextension.
  • Step 5: Exhale as you slowly lower the bar back to the ground, maintaining control and form.

Note: Focus on maintaining a neutral spine throughout the lift. Use proper technique and avoid rounding your back to prevent injury.
