Pull-Ups

Pull-Ups are a challenging and effective exercise that targets the upper body, focusing on the back, shoulders, and arms.

  • Builds strength in the lats, biceps, and shoulders.
  • Improves grip strength and overall upper body endurance.
  • Enhances functional strength for lifting and pulling movements.
  • Engages core muscles to improve stability and posture.
  • Can be adapted for progressive strength training with variations.
  • Step 1: Stand below a pull-up bar and grip it with your palms facing away from you (overhand grip) and hands shoulder-width apart.
  • Step 2: Engage your core, and pull your shoulders back and down to start the movement.
  • Step 3: Pull yourself up until your chin is above the bar, keeping your body straight and controlled.
  • Step 4: Lower yourself back down slowly to the starting position, fully extending your arms.
  • Step 5: Perform 5-10 repetitions, depending on your fitness level, ensuring proper form.
  • Step 6: Rest briefly and repeat for 2-3 sets or as desired.

Note: If you are new to pull-ups, use an assisted pull-up machine, resistance bands, or perform negative pull-ups to build strength.
