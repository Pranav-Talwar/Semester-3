HIIT Training

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) is a cardiovascular exercise strategy alternating short bursts of intense activity with periods of rest or lower-intensity exercises.

  • Burns more calories in less time compared to traditional workouts.
  • Improves cardiovascular health and endurance.
  • Boosts metabolism and aids in fat loss.
  • Enhances muscle strength and flexibility.
  • Suitable for various fitness levels with modifiable intensity.
  • Step 1: Warm up with light cardio exercises like jogging or jumping jacks for 5 minutes.
  • Step 2: Perform an intense activity like sprinting, burpees, or cycling for 30–45 seconds at full effort.
  • Step 3: Follow with 15–30 seconds of rest or low-intensity activity such as walking.
  • Step 4: Repeat this cycle for 10–20 minutes, depending on your fitness level.
  • Step 5: Cool down with stretching or light jogging for 5 minutes to relax your muscles.

Note: Consult with a fitness professional if you're new to HIIT. Gradually increase the intensity and duration to avoid injury and maximize benefits.
