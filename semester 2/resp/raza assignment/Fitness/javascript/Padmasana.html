Padmasana (Lotus Pose)

Padmasana, or the Lotus Pose, is a classic meditative posture in yoga that helps calm the mind and enhance focus.

  • Improves posture and strengthens the back and spine.
  • Enhances flexibility in the hips and knees.
  • Promotes mental relaxation and reduces stress.
  • Stimulates abdominal organs and aids digestion.
  • Encourages mindfulness and deep breathing.
  • Step 1: Sit on the ground with your legs extended and spine straight.
  • Step 2: Bend your right knee and place your right foot on your left thigh, sole facing upward.
  • Step 3: Repeat with your left foot, placing it on your right thigh.
  • Step 4: Rest your hands on your knees, palms facing upward, and form a mudra if desired.
  • Step 5: Close your eyes, relax your body, and focus on your breathing.
  • Step 6: Hold the pose for 1-5 minutes, gradually increasing the duration as your flexibility improves.

Note: Avoid this pose if you have recent knee or hip injuries. Use a cushion or folded blanket for additional support if necessary.
