Squats

Squats are a compound lower-body exercise that targets the quads, hamstrings, glutes, and core, making them an essential movement for strength and mobility.

  • Builds lower body strength and muscle mass, particularly in the thighs, hips, and glutes.
  • Improves mobility and flexibility in the hips, knees, and ankles.
  • Boosts core strength and stability, promoting good posture.
  • Enhances athletic performance by developing power in the legs and hips.
  • Helps with overall calorie burning and fat loss when incorporated into a workout routine.
  • Step 1: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, with your toes slightly turned out. Keep your back straight and your chest lifted.
  • Step 2: Push your hips back and lower your body by bending your knees, as if you are sitting into a chair. Make sure your knees don't extend beyond your toes.
  • Step 3: Lower down until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as low as you can go while maintaining good form.
  • Step 4: Press through your heels to push yourself back up to the starting position, straightening your legs and returning to a standing posture.
  • Step 5: Repeat for 8-12 repetitions, depending on your fitness level, focusing on controlled movements and proper alignment throughout.

Note: If you're new to squats, start with bodyweight squats and focus on perfecting your form before adding resistance. Avoid rounding your back during the movement to prevent injury.
